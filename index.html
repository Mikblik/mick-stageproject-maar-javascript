RA Trajectory Predictor

Welcome to the RA Trajectory Predictor. This tool helps to track how Rheumatoid Arthritis develops over time. After you load your data, you can look at the results in two different ways:

  • Single Patient View: Look closely at one specific patient. You can see their test results over time, check the charts, and see the top 5 most similar historical patients.
  • All Patients View: Look at all patients together. It shows a map of how groups of patients move from one disease stage to another.

8 Disease Stages (L1 - L8)

Each visit of a patient represents a single moment in time. Based on the lab results of that specific visit, the tool calculates one of the 8 disease stages (L1 to L8).

4 Trajectories (TR1 - TR4)

When a patient has multiple visits over time, these stages form a path. The tool uses this timeline to predict one of the 4 disease trajectories (TR1 to TR4).

Two Ways to Enter Data

You can upload a CSV file to look at a large group of patients at the same time, or you can use the Manual Input form below to check a single patient right now. You can change the calculation settings at the top right (⚙).

Documentation

The models, disease stages and disease trajectories used in this tool are based on the following scientific paper:

"Time-independent disease state identification defines distinct trajectories determined by localised vs systemic inflammation in patients with early rheumatoid arthritis"
